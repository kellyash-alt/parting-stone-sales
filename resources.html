Call Scripts

Guides for every stage of the conversation

Opening

"Hi [Name], my name is [Your Name] calling from Parting Stone. Do you have about two minutes? I'd love to share something that a number of funeral homes in [region] have been really excited about."

The Pitch

"We work with funeral homes to offer families something new after cremation — we solidify cremated remains into a set of smooth, natural stones. Families keep the stones rather than an urn, and they're finding it to be a much more tangible and personal way to stay connected."

The Ask

"We don't ask you to do anything other than mention it as an option. You set the price point, we handle everything else. Would you be open to a 15-minute call this week?"

If They Push Back

Thank them, ask to send a brief email, and note in CRM to follow up in 30 days.

Objection Handling

Empathetic responses that keep conversations moving
Objection Suggested Response
"We already offer urns — we have enough options." Parting Stone isn't an alternative to urns, it's an addition for families who want something more personal. It tends to appeal to a different type of family than traditional cremation products.
"How does our staff explain this to families?" We provide one-page materials you can leave in your arrangement room. Most directors say a sentence or two and families take it from there. We're also available to answer family questions directly.
"What does it cost the family?" You set the price. We give you wholesale pricing and you mark it up however makes sense for your market. Most partners price it between $495 and $895 retail.
"I'm not sure my families would go for this." It tends to resonate most with families who feel conflicted about traditional cremation. Partners are often surprised. Would it help to hear what a similar funeral home in your area has experienced?
"Is this safe? Are they really the remains?" Yes. The remains are processed with a strict chain-of-custody at our facility. Every stone set is uniquely tied to that individual. We can share our process documentation anytime.
"I'd need to talk to my owner first." Of course. Would it help if I put together a short summary you could share, or would you prefer a brief call with all of you together? Either works for me.
"We've tried add-ons and families don't buy them." The funeral homes with the highest adoption mention it as a natural part of the arrangement conversation — not as an upsell. We can talk through exactly how other partners approach it.
"Just email me information." Absolutely, I'll send that today. Would a 10-minute call next week make sense so I can answer any questions? I want to make sure it's useful for you, not just another email in the inbox.
